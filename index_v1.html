The B Corridor is an ecological corridor running between the Newtown and Ballygunner areas of Waterford City, connecting the habitats of the Lower River Suir SAC and the Knockboy Wetlands.

About the B Corridor

A community collective for biodiversity since 2024

Community-Led Conservation

Established in 2024, the B Corridor Group is a community collective dedicated to protecting and enhancing biodiversity. Its members include local residents, residents' associations, St Joseph's, St Benildus and St Mary's Parish Eco-Action Group, Waterford Trees for Life, Waterford City and County Council, and the National Biodiversity Data Centre.

Enhancing Habitats & Communities

The B Corridor Group aims to enhance habitats and communities along the corridor through educational events and biodiversity initiatives such as the planting of native trees, hedgerows, and pollinator-friendly patches across Waterford's Newtown to Ballygunner ecological route.

The B Corridor Route

Our ecological corridor stretches from Newtown to Ballygunner, encompassing the Lower River Suir SAC and the Knockboy Wetlands — a vital link for wildlife and community alike.
